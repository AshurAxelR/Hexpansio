Happiness

Happiness is calculated per city.

Each city starts with baseline happiness specified in new game settings (+5 by default).
Utopia upgrade increases this number by +1 each time it is built.

Additional Happiness can be gained as income from tiles and resources.

Happiness is reduced by:

  • City population.

    Highrise upgrade reduces this factor by 50%.

  • Number of cities.

    You eventually need to build Utopia in every city in order to continue expansion.

  • The number of unemployed workers.

    Unemployement reduces happiness quadratically.

  • Each consecutive turn spent in poverty (no ⬤ Gold in treasury).

  • Starvation (negative Food in the city).

    Starvation reduces happiness proportional to the city population.

  • Immigration.

  • The Void.

Levels of Happiness

  • Happy (Happiness is positive)

    gives Void Resistance within city borders proportional to Happiness. Happiness of +10 or more gives 100% resistance.

  • Content (Happiness is 0)

    gives no bonuses.

  • Unhappy (Happiness is negative)

    gives 50% penalty to city growth and 25% penalty to production.

  • Anrgy (-1 Happiness per population or lower)

    gives 50% penalty to production and stops city growth.

  • Raging (-2 Happiness per population or lower)

    stops all production and growth. The city will lose 1 population at the end of this turn.
